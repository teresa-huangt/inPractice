Attempting to use a weight that’s not available for a given typeface will cause those styles to default to the closest value. For example, the Times New Roman typeface comes in two weights: normal, or 400, and bold, or 700. Attempting to use a weight of 900 will default the typeface to the closest related weight, 700 in this case.
